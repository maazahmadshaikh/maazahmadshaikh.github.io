## Databases & Websites

Key resources for language documentation and research include:

- [Glottolog](https://glottolog.org/){:target="_blank"}: A comprehensive reference for the world's languages, offering detailed information on lesser-known languages.
- [Ethnologue](https://www.ethnologue.com/){:target="_blank"}: Provides statistics and detailed information on living languages globally.
- [Grammar Watch](https://linguistic-typology.org/grammarwatch/){:target="_blank"}: A resource for finding, reading, and citing open-access grammars.
- [Databases by Association for Linguistic Typology](https://linguistic-typology.org/databases/){:target="_blank"}: A collection of databases for linguistic research.
- [The E-MELD School of Best Practices in Language Documentation](http://emeld.org/school/index.html){:target="_blank"}: Offers guidance on various aspects of language documentation.
- [Journal of Language Survey Reports](https://www.sil.org/resources/publications/jlsr){:target="_blank"}: Access to SIL's sociolinguistic surveys.
- [MPI EVA Tools for Language Description](https://www.eva.mpg.de/lingua/tools-at-lingboard/tools.php){:target="_blank"}: Tools for field linguistics and language description.
- [Archiving for the Future](https://archivingforthefuture.teachable.com/){:target="_blank"}: An educational resource for archiving in language documentation.

- [The Foundation for Endangered Languages (FEL)](http://www.ogmios.org/grants/){:target="_blank"}: Supports projects that document and protect endangered languages.
- [Firebird Foundation for Anthropological Research (FFAR)](https://www.firebirdfellowships.org/){:target="_blank"}: Grants for anthropological and linguistic research.
- [Wenner-Gren Foundation for Anthropological Research (WGFAR)](http://www.wennergren.org/programs/dissertation-fieldwork-grants){:target="_blank"}: Dissertation fieldwork grants.

## Language Archives

### International Archives

- [Endangered Languages Archive (ELAR)](https://www.elararchive.org/){:target="_blank"}: A digital repository for endangered language documentation materials.
- [Documentation of Endangered Languages (DoBeS)](http://dobes.mpi.nl/){:target="_blank"}: Hosted by the Max Planck Institute.
- [Pangloss](https://pangloss.cnrs.fr/?lang=en){:target="_blank"}: An archive for languages and oral traditions, hosted by CNRS, France.
- [Rosetta Project](http://rosettaproject.org/archive/collection){:target="_blank"}: Aims to develop a contemporary version of the historic Rosetta Stone.

### Areal Archives

Regional archives focusing on specific geographical areas:

- [Alaska Native Language Archive](http://www.uaf.edu/anla/){:target="_blank"}: Preserves and shares documentation of Alaska Native languages.
- [California Language Archive](https://cla.berkeley.edu/){:target="_blank"}: A repository for materials on the languages of California and the Americas.
- [Archive of the Indigenous Languages of Latin America (AILLA)](http://www.ailla.utexas.org/site/welcome.html){:target="_blank"}: Dedicated to the preservation of indigenous languages of Latin America.
- [Pacific And Regional Archive for Digital Sources in Endangered Cultures (PARADISEC)](https://www.paradisec.org.au/){:target="_blank"}: Focuses on the digital preservation of research materials from the Pacific and surrounding regions.
- [Kaipuleohone Language Archive](http://ling.hawaii.edu/kaipuleohone-language-archive/){:target="_blank"}: University of Hawaii's digital language archive, focusing on the Pacific and Asia.
- [LangueDOC](http://www.philol.msu.ru/~languedoc/eng/archives/index.php){:target="_blank"}: An archive for languages of Russia and neighboring countries.
- [African Language Materials Archive (ALMA)](http://alma.matrix.msu.edu/){:target="_blank"}: A digital archive for African language materials.
- [The Computational Resource for South Asian Languages (CoRSAL)](https://corsal.unt.edu/){:target="_blank"}: Archive focusing on South Asian languages.
- [Sikkim-Darjeeling Himalayas Endangered Language Archive (SiDHELA)](http://14.139.206.50:8080/jspui/handle/1/6541){:target="_blank"}: An archive for endangered languages in the Sikkim-Darjeeling Himalayan region.

## Software and Applications

Tools and software commonly used in language documentation:

- [FLEx (FieldWorks Language Explorer)](https://software.sil.org/fieldworks/){:target="_blank"}: A tool for linguistic analysis and data management, particularly useful for managing lexicons and interlinearizing texts.
- [ELAN](https://archive.mpi.nl/tla/elan){:target="_blank"}: An annotation tool for audio and video recordings, allowing for detailed transcription and analysis of linguistic data.
- [SayMore](https://software.sil.org/saymore/){:target="_blank"}: Assists in managing and annotating language documentation materials, streamlining the process of preparing data for archiving.
- [Lameta](https://sites.google.com/site/metadatatooldiscussion/home?authuser=0){:target="_blank"}: A metadata tool designed to help documenters create, edit, and manage metadata for language resources.
- [Field Linguist's Toolbox](https://software.sil.org/toolbox/){:target="_blank"}: A data management and analysis tool for field linguists, useful for managing dictionaries, texts, and cultural data.
- [Phonology Assistant](https://software.sil.org/phonologyassistant/){:target="_blank"}: A tool for analyzing phonetic and phonological data.
- [Webonary](https://www.webonary.org/){:target="_blank"}: Provides a platform to publish dictionaries online, making them easily accessible.
- [Praat](https://www.fon.hum.uva.nl/praat/){:target="_blank"}: A software for phonetic analysis of speech, including speech synthesis and manipulation.
- [Lexique Pro](https://software.sil.org/lexiquepro/){:target="_blank"}: A tool for creating, managing, and publishing dictionaries.
- [IPA Keyboard](https://scripts.sil.org/cms/scripts/page.php?site_id=nrsi&id=UniIPAKeyboard){:target="_blank"}: A tool for typing International Phonetic Alphabet symbols.
- [IPA Help](https://ipahelp.languagetechnology.org/Home%20Page.html){:target="_blank"}: A software for learning and teaching the pronunciation of IPA symbols.
- [Comparalex](http://comparalex.org/index.php?page=query){:target="_blank"}: A database for comparing lexical data across languages.
- [Transcriber](http://trans.sourceforge.net/en/presentation.php){:target="_blank"}: A tool for segmenting, labeling, and transcribing speech.
- [Other SIL Products](https://software.sil.org/products/){:target="_blank"}: A range of software products for language documentation and analysis.
- [Audacity](https://sourceforge.net/projects/audacity/){:target="_blank"}: A free, open-source, cross-platform audio software for recording and editing sounds.
- [CuPED](http://christophercox.ca/cuped/){:target="_blank"}: A tool for aligning and analyzing elicited linguistic data.
