My Background

Early Life and Education

I was born and brought up in Mumbai, India. My ancestral family hails from the Azamgarh district of Uttar Pradesh. I did my schooling at St. Dominic Savio High School and my Intermediate Education (10+2) at Mithibai College, Mumbai. I have a BE in Mechanical Engineering from Dwarkadas J. Sanghvi College of Engineering, Mumbai.

Passion for Languages

During my undergraduate days, I developed a strong affinity and passion for languages, focusing on my mother tongue Urdu, or more generally, Hindustani. This led me to the field of 'Linguistics' or 'Language Science'.

Master's Program in Linguistics

After completing my undergrad degree, I joined the Master's program in Linguistics at the University of Delhi (DU), Delhi. Here, I was trained by notable linguists and educators, including Dr. Gail Coelho, Prof. Tista Bagchi, and others. My focus was on lesser-explored languages and language documentation.

Research and Fieldwork

  • Documenting the Ladakhi language with consultants Jigmet Dorje and Nilza Angmo, culminating in my MA thesis titled 'Interrogation in Ladakhi'.
  • Exploring Western Tibetic languages, leading to my choice of Balti for further research.
  • Working on Eastern Purik and Sham Ladakhi during my M. Phil. program at Jawaharlal Nehru University under Prof. Pradeep Kumar Das.
  • Documenting my heritage language, Azamgarhi, and conducting dialect surveys on Bhojpuri.

Current Pursuits and Doctoral Research

I am currently writing my M. Phil. thesis, titled 'Verb Morphology in Awadhi of Azamgarh', and pursuing doctoral research on Zangskari, a Western Tibetic language.
